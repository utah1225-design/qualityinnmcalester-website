10 Best Things to Do in McAlester, OK in 2026

Quality Inn & Suites McAlesterFebruary 1, 202610 min read

McAlester surprises people. Tucked into the green hills of southeast Oklahoma where Hwy 69 meets Hwy 270, it mixes small-town Oklahoma charm with a few genuinely one-of-a-kind attractions — a world-famous Italian restaurant, a castle-like Masonic temple, and some of the best lake and state-park country in the state, all within easy reach. Whether you're here for a weekend, passing through on a road trip, or settling in for a work rotation, here are the ten things worth your time.

1. Tour the Scottish Rite Masonic Temple

The single most striking building in McAlester. The Scottish Rite Masonic Temple is a massive, ornate landmark that looks like it was lifted out of a European capital and set down in Oklahoma. Its architecture, grand auditorium, and detailed interiors make it a genuine bucket-list stop for anyone interested in history, design, or just impressive buildings. Check ahead for tour availability.

2. Eat at Pete's Place in Krebs

Just a few minutes from McAlester in the little town of Krebs — Oklahoma's "Little Italy" — sits Pete's Place, serving family-style Italian dinners since 1925. This is the meal people drive hours for: hand-made ravioli, lamb fries, and the famous family-style spread. If you do one sit-down dinner during your stay, make it this one.

3. Explore Robbers Cave State Park

About a half-hour north near Wilburton, Robbers Cave State Park is a southeast Oklahoma classic — sandstone cliffs, hiking trails, fishing lakes, and the legendary cave system said to have sheltered outlaws like Belle Starr and the Younger Gang. It's a perfect half- or full-day trip for hikers, climbers, and families.

4. Spend a Day at Lake Eufaula

About 35 minutes east is Lake Eufaula, the largest lake in Oklahoma by surface area. Boating, fishing, swimming beaches, and lakeside dining make it the go-to outdoor escape from McAlester. We wrote a whole Lake Eufaula day trip guide if you want the full plan.

5. Try Your Luck at Choctaw Casino McAlester

A short drive south on Hwy 69, Choctaw Casino McAlester offers gaming, dining, and live entertainment. It's an easy evening out without leaving town, and a popular stop for travelers looking for a little nightlife after a day on the road.

6. Catch an Event at the Southeast Expo Center

McAlester's Southeast Expo Center, just a few miles west on Hwy 270, hosts livestock shows, rodeos, trade shows, fairs, and community events throughout the year. If your visit lines up with an event, it's a great window into local life — see our Expo Center events guide for what to expect and how to plan lodging around it.

7. Walk Historic Downtown McAlester

Downtown McAlester rewards a slow stroll — historic brick storefronts, local shops, murals, and cafés. It's the kind of walkable main-street district that gives you a real feel for the town's character, and a nice low-key way to spend a morning.

8. Sample the Local Food Scene

Beyond Pete's Place, McAlester's dining runs deep for a town this size — barbecue, steakhouses, Mexican, Chinese, classic American diners, and the Krebs Italian institutions just down the road. Our dining guide maps out what's close to Hwy 69, much of it within a mile or two of the hotel.

9. Take the Hwy 69 Scenic Drive

The stretch of US Highway 69 through southeast Oklahoma is a genuinely pretty drive — rolling hills, lakes, and small towns with their own stories. Whether you're headed toward Lake Eufaula to the north or the Choctaw Nation country to the south, the road itself is part of the experience. More in our Hwy 69 road trip guide.

10. Use McAlester as Your Southeast Oklahoma Base Camp

This is the move savvy travelers make: McAlester sits at the crossroads of Hwy 69 and Hwy 270, which puts Robbers Cave, Lake Eufaula, the Choctaw Nation, and the Ouachita foothills all within an easy drive. Stay central, sleep well, and day-trip out in every direction.
