The Problem Every Project Manager Faces

You've got 8 guys arriving at 5:30 PM after a long drive. Here's what usually happens at most hotels:

The bad version: Crews show up. Front desk has "rooms available." But Sam needs a kitchenette (he cooks for the crew), Mike requested ground floor (bad knees), and three guys booked doubles but got singles. Your phone starts ringing at 6:15 PM.

The good version: You sent a rooming list to Garry the day before. Every room is blocked. Sam's kitchenette is waiting. Mike's ground-floor room is prepped. Check-in takes 8 minutes. No surprises. Crew focuses on the job, not on hotel problems.

We do the second one.

How It Actually Works: The Rooming List Process

Here's our process—nothing complicated, just reliable and proven:

Step 1: You Send the List

Email or text Garry your crew rooming list before arrival. Include:

  • Number of guests and crew size
  • Specific room requests (kitchenette, ground floor, accessible, double beds, etc.)
  • Preferred check-in time window
  • Group billing code (if applicable) or crew name

Easiest way: Submit your crew request online → (2-minute form) or email qualitymcalester@gmail.com

Step 2: We Confirm & Block

Garry blocks every requested room. No overbooking. No surprises at check-in. If a request can't be honored, he tells you immediately—not when your crew walks in tired at 6 PM. This is where most hotels fail. We confirm in advance.

Step 3: Pre-Coordinated Check-In

We brief the front desk on crew arrival. Drivers don't fumble for credit cards. Staff know exactly who's coming and what rooms are assigned. When your crew shows up, check-in is fast. Crew gets to their rooms and settles in.

Step 4: Group Billing (Optional)

If you're billing crews directly, we set up group codes. Drivers use the code instead of personal cards. You get one consolidated bill at the end. Clean, simple, audit-friendly.

That's it. But it changes everything because your crew stops being a transaction for the front desk—and starts being an expected, managed part of the day.
