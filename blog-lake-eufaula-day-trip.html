Visiting Lake Eufaula from McAlester: A Day Trip Guide

Quality Inn & Suites McAlesterOctober 5, 20258 min read

Ask anyone in McAlester where to spend a warm day, and Lake Eufaula comes up fast. About 35 minutes east of town sits the largest lake in Oklahoma by surface area — more than 100,000 acres of open water, coves, marinas, and shoreline. It's the easiest big outdoor escape from McAlester, and it makes a perfect day trip. Here's how to plan one.

Getting There

From McAlester, Lake Eufaula is a straightforward drive of roughly 35 minutes heading east. The lake is huge and has many access points — marinas, state park areas, swimming beaches, and boat ramps spread around its shoreline — so decide what you want to do first, then pick the access point that fits. Mornings are ideal: you beat the heat and the crowds, and you get the calm water.

What to Do at Lake Eufaula

Get on the water

This is the main event. Boating, jet-skiing, kayaking, and paddleboarding are all popular. If you don't have your own boat, look into marina rentals around the lake. The sheer size of Eufaula means there's always a quiet cove to find.

Fishing

Lake Eufaula is a well-known fishing destination — largemouth bass, crappie, catfish, and sand bass all draw anglers. Bring your Oklahoma fishing license and your gear, or book a local guide if you want to skip the guesswork.

Swim and relax on the beach

Several areas offer swimming beaches and shaded picnic spots — perfect for families or anyone who'd rather plant a chair in the sand than pilot a boat. Pack a cooler and make an afternoon of it.

Hike and explore the state park areas

The Lake Eufaula State Park areas offer trails, scenic overlooks, and wildlife. It's a good way to stretch your legs and see the lake from above.

Eat lakeside

Several spots around the lake serve food with a view. A lakeside lunch is the natural midpoint of a good Eufaula day.

What to Bring

  • Sunscreen and shade — Oklahoma sun on open water is no joke.
  • Plenty of water and a cooler — pack drinks and snacks; you can stock up at the Walmart Supercenter next to the hotel before you head out.
  • Swimsuit, towel, and a change of clothes.
  • Fishing license and gear if you plan to fish.
  • Cash for marinas, bait, and small vendors.
  • A dry bag or cooler for phones and valuables if you're getting on the water.

Make It a Two-Day Trip

A single day at Eufaula is great — but the lake plus everything else around McAlester (Robbers Cave, the Krebs Italian restaurants, downtown, the Scottish Rite Temple) easily fills a weekend. Basing yourself in McAlester means you're central to all of it, with a comfortable room to come back to after a sunny day on the water. See our 10 things to do in McAlester for the full weekend.
